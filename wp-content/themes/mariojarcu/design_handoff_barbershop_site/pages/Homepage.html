The barber

Trained in Romania.
Honed on the floor.

Mario learned the trade in Romania and sharpened it in some of the most exacting barbershops in the business — the kind where a fade is measured in millimetres and a shave is a ritual, not a rush.

Today he works for himself, one chair, no assistants, no shortcuts. He doesn't advertise. He's booked weeks out all the same — because the work speaks, and the people he cuts for keep sending their friends.

“One chair means you get all of my attention — and none of the rush.”
